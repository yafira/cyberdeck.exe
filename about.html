about this moodboard

cyberdeck.exe is a working moodboard gathered by yafira and alanna as we prepare to apply for SOFTER's residency.

it pulls live from an are.na channel where we collect soft systems, cyberdeck inspo, and the small experiments that feed our thinking. every block you see here was added there first.

who we are

Alanna Okun

b. 1990, Boston, MA  //  @alannabean

Alanna Okun is a writer, textile artist, and game designer. Her work concerns the connections between craft, creativity, and computation.

A lifelong knitter and largely self-taught programmer, Okun's most recent projects center on electronics, textiles, and interactive storytelling (often at the same time). She makes small, personal video games exploring what it means to be a person in the world, and uses handmaking practices, particularly knitting, to understand and interrogate code, circuitry, and digital technology.

She is the author of two books of nonfiction: The Curse of the Boyfriend Sweater: Essays on Crafting (Macmillan, 2018) and Knit a Hat: A Beginner's Guide to Knitting (Abrams, 2020). Professionally, Okun spent over a decade working as an essays and features editor for publications including BuzzFeed, Racked, and Vox. Based in New York since 2008, she is a graduate of NYU ITP.

Yafira Martinez

b. 1990, NYC, New York  //  @electrocutelab

Yafira Martinez is a design engineer and creative technologist working across physical computing, embedded systems, and experimental interface design. Her practice explores how technology can move beyond conventional screens and rigid devices into softer, more tactile, and materially expressive forms.

With a B.S. in Computer Science and a master's degree from NYU's Interactive Telecommunications Program (ITP), Yafira develops projects that integrate hardware, software, and material exploration. She often works with textile-based electronics and soft circuits, approaching computation as something that can be shaped, stitched, and felt.

Her work is grounded in calm technology and poetic computation, exploring modes of interaction shaped by attention, rhythm, and presence. Through this lens, she builds systems that reconsider how technology is experienced, with an emphasis on care, perception, and embodied engagement.
